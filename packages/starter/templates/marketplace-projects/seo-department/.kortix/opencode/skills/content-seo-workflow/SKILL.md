# Content SEO Workflow

Use this skill for content briefs, refreshes, editorial planning, internal links,
and organic landing-page recommendations.

## Topic selection

Start with business fit, then search demand. A good topic has:

- a clear audience problem,
- a conversion or trust-building path,
- realistic ranking angle,
- evidence from first-party data or SERP research,
- a page type that matches intent.

## Brief structure

Every brief should include:

- target audience and job-to-be-done,
- primary intent and funnel stage,
- title options,
- recommended URL slug,
- outline,
- key questions to answer,
- proof points needed,
- internal links to add,
- competitor gaps,
- conversion path,
- risks or claims needing review.

## Refresh structure

For existing pages:

1. Preserve the sections that still satisfy intent.
2. Remove stale claims, broken examples, and outdated screenshots.
3. Add missing subtopics discovered from SERP and competitor review.
4. Improve titles, descriptions, headings, snippets, tables, FAQs, and media
   only when they help the searcher.
5. Add internal links to and from relevant priority pages.
6. Mark anything that needs legal, product, medical, financial, or executive
   review.

## Quality rules

No spun pages, doorway pages, keyword stuffing, fake reviews, unsupported
claims, or filler sections. The page should be useful even if rankings did not
exist.

## Output

Return the brief or refresh plan, then a compact backlog row:
priority, page/topic, expected impact, evidence, owner, approval needed, and
next step.
